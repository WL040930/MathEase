keyboard_arrow_right 2.1.1 What are factors?

Factors

  • Factors - Factors are numbers that can be multiplied together to get another number. For example, the factors of 12 are 1, 2, 3, 4, 6, and 12 because 1 × 12 = 12, 2 × 6 = 12, and 3 × 4 = 12.

Example

(a) You're trying to determine if a number is a factor of 60 and list all the factors of 60. Let's say the number you are considering is 15. Follow the steps to determine if it is a factor and find all the factors of 60.

Step 1: Identify the number and the target number:

  • Consider the number: 15.
  • Target number: 60.

Step 2: Determine if the number is a factor of the target number:

  • A number is a factor of another if the target number can be divided by this number without leaving a remainder.
  • Check if 60 divided by 15 leaves no remainder.
  • Calculation: 60 ÷ 15 = 4, which is an integer with no remainder. So, 15 is a factor of 60.

Step 3: List all the factors of the target number:

  • Factors of 60 are all the numbers that divide 60 without leaving a remainder.
  • Start with 1 and proceed up to 60: 1, 2, 3, 4, 5, 6, 10, 12, 15, 20, 30, 60.

Step 4: Final summary:

  • 15 is a factor of 60.
  • All factors of 60 are: 1, 2, 3, 4, 5, 6, 10, 12, 15, 20, 30, 60.

keyboard_arrow_right 2.1.2 What are prime factors?

Prime Factors

  • Prime Factors - Prime factors are the prime numbers that multiply together to give the original number. For example, the prime factors of 12 are 2 and 3 because 2 × 2 × 3 = 12.

Figure 2.1.2.1: Example of Prime Factors


Figure 2.1.2.2: Repeated Division and Factor Tree

Example

(a) You have a number and want to identify its prime factors. Let's say the number is 60. Follow the steps to determine its prime factors using repeated division and the factor method.

Step 1: Identify the number:

  • Consider the number: 60.

Step 2: Use repeated division to find the prime factors:

  • Start dividing by the smallest prime number, 2.
  • 60 ÷ 2 = 30
  • 30 ÷ 2 = 15
  • 15 is not divisible by 2, move to the next prime number, 3.
  • 15 ÷ 3 = 5
  • 5 is a prime number and cannot be divided further.

Step 3: Express the number as a product of prime factors:

  • From the repeated divisions, the prime factors of 60 are 2, 2, 3, and 5.
  • So, 60 can be expressed as 2 × 2 × 3 × 5.

Step 4: Final expression:

  • The prime factorization of 60 is 2² × 3 × 5.

keyboard_arrow_right 2.1.3 What are common factors?

Common Factors

  • Common Factors - Common factors are numbers that divide two or more numbers without leaving a remainder. For example, the common factors of 12 and 18 are 1, 2, 3, and 6 because they divide both numbers without leaving a remainder.

Figure 2.1.3.1: Determine Whether the Number is One of the Common Factors


Figure 2.1.3.2: List Out all the Common Factors

Example

(a) Imagine you're organizing a school event and need to determine if a number is a common factor of two other numbers. Let's say the numbers are 36 and 60, and you want to check if 6 is a common factor. Follow the steps to determine if it is and list all the common factors of 36 and 60.

Step 1: Identify the numbers:

  • First number: 36.
  • Second number: 60.
  • Number to check: 6.

Step 2: Determine if the number is a common factor:

  • A number is a common factor if it divides both numbers without leaving a remainder.
  • Check if 36 divided by 6 leaves no remainder: 36 ÷ 6 = 6 (no remainder).
  • Check if 60 divided by 6 leaves no remainder: 60 ÷ 6 = 10 (no remainder).
  • Since 6 divides both 36 and 60 without a remainder, 6 is a common factor.

Step 3: List all the common factors:

  • Find all factors of 36: 1, 2, 3, 4, 6, 9, 12, 18, 36.
  • Find all factors of 60: 1, 2, 3, 4, 5, 6, 10, 12, 15, 20, 30, 60.
  • Common factors of 36 and 60 are: 1, 2, 3, 6, 12.

Step 4: Final summary:

  • 6 is a common factor of 36 and 60.
  • All common factors of 36 and 60 are: 1, 2, 3, 6, 12.

keyboard_arrow_right 2.1.4 what is highest common factor (HCF)?

Highest Common Factor (HCF)

  • Highest Common Factor (HCF) - The highest common factor (HCF) is the largest number that divides two or more numbers without leaving a remainder. For example, the HCF of 12 and 18 is 6 because 6 is the largest number that divides both 12 and 18 without leaving a remainder.

Figure 2.1.4.1: Methods to Determine Highest Common Factor (HCF)

Example

(a) Consider the numbers 48 and 72. Determine the highest common factor (HCF) of these numbers by listing all factors, using repeated division, and prime factorization.

Step 1: List All Factors:

  • Factors of 48: 1, 2, 3, 4, 6, 8, 12, 16, 24, 48.
  • Factors of 72: 1, 2, 3, 4, 6, 8, 9, 12, 18, 24, 36, 72.
  • Common factors of 48 and 72: 1, 2, 3, 4, 6, 8, 12, 24.
  • The highest common factor (HCF) is 24.

Step 2: Determine HCF Using Repeated Division:

  • Start with the smaller number, 48, and divide both numbers by their common prime factors.
  • 48 ÷ 2 = 24, 72 ÷ 2 = 36.
  • 24 ÷ 2 = 12, 36 ÷ 2 = 18.
  • 12 ÷ 2 = 6, 18 ÷ 2 = 9.
  • 6 ÷ 3 = 2, 9 ÷ 3 = 3.
  • No more common factors other than 1, so the product of the common prime factors is 2 × 2 × 2 × 3 = 24.
  • The HCF using repeated division is 24.

Step 3: Determine HCF Using Prime Factorization:

  • Prime factorization of 48: 2 × 2 × 2 × 2 × 3 = 2⁴ × 3.
  • Prime factorization of 72: 2 × 2 × 2 × 3 × 3 = 2³ × 3².
  • Common prime factors: 2 × 2 × 2 × 3 = 2³ × 3.
  • The HCF using prime factorization is 2³ × 3 = 24.

Step 4: Final summary:

  • The highest common factor (HCF) of 48 and 72 is 24.

keyboard_arrow_right 2.2.1 What are common multiples?

Common Mutiples

  • Common Multiples - Common multiples are numbers that are multiples of two or more numbers. For example, the common multiples of 3 and 4 are 12, 24, 36, 48, and so on because they are multiples of both 3 and 4.

Example

(a) Consider the numbers 12 and 18. How do you determine if 36 is a common multiple of these numbers? Additionally, list out all the common multiples of 12 and 18 up to 100.

Step 1: Identify the numbers:

  • First number: 12.
  • Second number: 18.
  • Number to check: 36.

Step 2: Determine if the number is a common multiple:

  • A number is a common multiple if it is divisible by both given numbers without leaving a remainder.
  • Check if 36 divided by 12 leaves no remainder: 36 ÷ 12 = 3 (no remainder).
  • Check if 36 divided by 18 leaves no remainder: 36 ÷ 18 = 2 (no remainder).
  • Since 36 divides both 12 and 18 without a remainder, 36 is a common multiple.

Step 3: List all common multiples up to 100:

  • Multiples of 12: 12, 24, 36, 48, 60, 72, 84, 96.
  • Multiples of 18: 18, 36, 54, 72, 90.
  • Common multiples of 12 and 18 up to 100: 36, 72.

Step 4: Final summary:

  • 36 is a common multiple of 12 and 18.
  • All common multiples of 12 and 18 up to 100 are: 36, 72.

keyboard_arrow_right 2.2.2 What is lowest common multiple (LCM)?

Lowest Common Multiple (LCM)

  • Lowest Common Multiple (LCM) - The lowest common multiple (LCM) is the smallest number that is a multiple of two or more numbers. For example, the LCM of 4 and 6 is 12 because 12 is the smallest number that is a multiple of both 4 and 6.

Figure 2.2.2.1: Determine Lowest Common Multiple (LCM)

Example

(a) You're tasked with determining the least common factor (LCM) of two numbers: 24 and 36. How would you express this situation mathematically?

Step 1: Identify the numbers:

  • First number: 24.
  • Second number: 36.

Step 2: Define the expression for LCM:

  • The least common factor (LCM) of 24 and 36 can be expressed as "LCM(24, 36)".

Step 3: Explanation of LCM:

  • The LCM is the smallest number that is a factor of both 24 and 36.
  • To determine the LCM, we need to find the common factors of 24 and 36 and choose the smallest one.

Step 4: Find the LCM:

  • Factors of 24: 1, 2, 3, 4, 6, 8, 12, 24.
  • Factors of 36: 1, 2, 3, 4, 6, 9, 12, 18, 36.
  • Common factors: 1, 2, 3, 4, 6, 12.
  • The least common factor (LCM) of 24 and 36 is 1.

Step 5: Final summary:

  • The least common factor (LCM) of 24 and 36 is 1.
