keyboard_arrow_right 3.1.1 What are squares and perfect squares?

Smart Tips

  • A square of a number is the result of multiplying the number by itself. For example, the square of 5 is 25 because 5 x 5 = 25. The process of squaring a number is also called raising the number to the power of 2, or simply "squared."

  • Perfect squares are the squares of whole numbers. For instance, 1, 4, 9, 16, and 25 are perfect squares because they can be expressed as 1^2, 2^2, 3^2, 4^2, and 5^2 respectively.

Figure 3.1.1.1: Example of a Perfect Square

Properties of Perfect Squares:

  • The square of any positive integer is always a positive integer. Similarly, the square of any negative integer is also a positive integer because the product of two negative numbers is positive.

  • Perfect squares have an odd number of total divisors. For example, 36 is a perfect square and its divisors are 1, 2, 3, 4, 6, 9, 12, 18, and 36, making a total of 9 divisors.

Figure 3.1.1.2: Properties of Perfect Squares

Example

(a) Calculate the square of 7 and determine if it is a perfect square.

Step 1: Calculation:

  • Square of 7: 7 x 7 = 49.

Step 2: Determine if it is a perfect square:

  • Since 49 is the result of 7 x 7, it is a perfect square.

Explanation:

  • Square of 3: 3 x 3 = 9, which is a perfect square (3^2).
  • Square of 10: 10 x 10 = 100, which is a perfect square (10^2).
  • Square of 15: 15 x 15 = 225, which is a perfect square (15^2).
  • Square of 20: 20 x 20 = 400, which is a perfect square (20^2).

keyboard_arrow_right 3.1.2 How do you determine whether a number is a perfect square?

Smart Tips

  • A number is a perfect square if it can be expressed as the product of an integer with itself. For example, 36 is a perfect square because it can be written as 6 x 6.

  • To determine if a number is a perfect square, you can find its square root and check if the result is an integer. For instance, the square root of 36 is 6, which is an integer, so 36 is a perfect square.

Figure 3.1.2.1: Finding the Square Root to Determine Perfect Squares


Methods to Determine Perfect Squares:

  • Prime Factorization: Break down the number into its prime factors. If all prime factors appear in pairs, then the number is a perfect square. For example, the prime factorization of 36 is 2 x 2 x 3 x 3, which confirms it as a perfect square.

  • Last Digit Check: A perfect square will end in 0, 1, 4, 5, 6, or 9 in base 10. For example, the number 49 ends in 9, so it could be a perfect square.

  • Digital Root Method: The digital root of a perfect square can only be 0, 1, 4, 7, or 9. The digital root is found by repeatedly summing the digits of the number until a single digit is obtained. For example, the digital root of 49 is 4 + 9 = 13, and 1 + 3 = 4, which is valid for a perfect square.

Figure 3.1.2.2: Methods to Identify Perfect Squares

Example

(a) Determine if 144 is a perfect square using the prime factorization method.

Step-by-Step Solution:

  • Find the prime factors of 144: 144 = 2 x 2 x 2 x 2 x 3 x 3.

  • Group the prime factors into pairs: (2 x 2), (2 x 2), (3 x 3).

  • Since all prime factors are paired, 144 is a perfect square (12 x 12).


Additional Practice:

Determine if the following numbers are perfect squares:

  • 81
  • 121
  • 200
  • 256

Explanation:

  • 81 = 9 x 9, so 81 is a perfect square.
  • 121 = 11 x 11, so 121 is a perfect square.
  • 200 is not a perfect square because its prime factorization (2 x 2 x 2 x 5 x 5) does not have all pairs.
  • 256 = 16 x 16, so 256 is a perfect square.

keyboard_arrow_right 3.1.3 What is the relationship between squares and square roots?

Smart Tips

  • The square of a number is the result of multiplying that number by itself. For example, the square of 4 is 16 because 4 x 4 = 16.

  • The square root of a number is a value that, when multiplied by itself, gives the original number. For example, the square root of 16 is 4 because 4 x 4 = 16.

  • In mathematical notation, if x is a number, then its square is written as x², and its square root is written as √x. For instance, if x = 5, then 5² = 25 and √25 = 5.

Figure 3.1.3.1: Visualizing the Relationship between Squares and Square Roots


Properties of Squares and Square Roots:

  • The square of any positive number is always positive, and the square of any negative number is also positive. For example, (-3)² = 9.

  • Every positive number has two square roots: one positive (called the principal square root) and one negative. For instance, the square roots of 25 are 5 and -5.

  • The square root of zero is zero. Zero is the only number with one square root.

Example

(a) If the square of a number is 49, what are its square roots?

Step-by-Step Solution:

  • To find the square roots of 49, we look for numbers that multiply by themselves to give 49.

  • Since 7 x 7 = 49 and (-7) x (-7) = 49, the square roots of 49 are 7 and -7.

  • 144
  • 225

Explanation:

  • The square roots of 64 are 8 and -8.
  • The square roots of 100 are 10 and -10.
  • The square roots of 144 are 12 and -12.

keyboard_arrow_right 3.2.1 What are cubes and perfect cubes?

Smart Tips

  • Cubes and perfect cubes are fundamental concepts in mathematics related to the multiplication of numbers by themselves three times.

  • A cube of a number is obtained by multiplying the number by itself twice. Mathematically, the cube of a number x is represented as x³.

  • A perfect cube is a number that is the result of cubing an integer. In other words, it is the cube of an integer. For example, 8 is a perfect cube because 8 = 2³.

Figure 3.2.1: Cubes and Perfect Cubes Diagram

Examples:

  • The cube of 2 is 2 × 2 × 2 = 8, making 8 a perfect cube.
  • The cube of 3 is 3 × 3 × 3 = 27, making 27 a perfect cube.
  • The cube of 4 is 4 × 4 × 4 = 64, making 64 a perfect cube.

Properties of Perfect Cubes:

  • Perfect cubes always have integer cube roots.
  • The cube root of a perfect cube gives the original integer.

Example

(a) Find the cube of 5.

Step-by-Step Solution:

  • Identify the number to be cubed: 5.

  • Multiply the number by itself twice: 5 × 5 × 5.

  • Calculate the result: 5 × 5 × 5 = 125.

  • Therefore, the cube of 5 is 125.


Additional Practice:

Find the cubes of the following numbers:

  • 2
  • 3
  • 4

Explanation:

  • The cube of 2 is 2 × 2 × 2 = 8.
  • The cube of 3 is 3 × 3 × 3 = 27.
  • The cube of 4 is 4 × 4 × 4 = 64.

Smart Tips

  • To determine whether a number is a perfect cube, you need to check if it is the cube of an integer.

  • You can use the cube root operation to find the cube root of the given number. If the result is an integer, then the number is a perfect cube.

Steps to Determine Whether a Number is a Perfect Cube:

  1. Find the cube root of the given number.

  2. If the cube root is an integer, then the number is a perfect cube.

Determine whether 64 is a perfect cube.

Step-by-Step Solution:

  1. Find the cube root of 125: ∛125 = 5.

  2. Since the cube root is an integer (5), 125 is a perfect cube.

Understanding Cubes and Cube Roots

Cubes and cube roots are fundamental concepts in mathematics, particularly in algebra and arithmetic. Let's delve deeper into their relationship:

Cubes: Cubes are the result of multiplying a number by itself twice. When a number x is cubed, it is raised to the power of 3, denoted as x³, which is equal to x * x * x.

Cube Roots: On the other hand, cube roots are the inverse operation of cubes. The cube root of a number x, denoted as ∛x, is a value that, when multiplied by itself twice, results in x.

Relationship: The relationship between cubes and cube roots is inverse. If a number x is cubed to get x³, then the cube root of x³ is x. In mathematical terms:

If y = x³, then ∛y = x

This relationship illustrates how cubes and cube roots are interconnected. When we cube a number, we are finding its volume in a cubic space, while finding the cube root is akin to determining the length of a side of a cube with that volume.

Cubes and cube roots are extensively used in various mathematical problems, including geometry, algebraic equations, and calculus. They provide insights into spatial relationships, patterns, and solutions to complex equations.

Example

Let's explore a practical example to understand the relationship between cubes and cube roots:

Calculate the cube of 5 and find its cube root.

Step 1: Calculate the cube of 5:

5³ = 5 * 5 * 5 = 125

Step 2: Find the cube root of 125:

∛125 = 5

Understanding Cube Roots

Calculating the cube root of a number is the inverse operation of cubing a number. Let's explore the process:

Definition: The cube root of a number x is a value that, when multiplied by itself twice, results in x. It is denoted by ∛x.

Process: To determine the cube root of a number, you can use various methods, including:

  1. Estimation: Make an educated guess and refine it using trial and error.
  2. Prime Factorization: Decompose the number into its prime factors and use properties of exponents to find the cube root.
  3. Newton's Method: Use iterative approximation techniques to find an accurate estimate of the cube root.

Examples: Let's consider a few examples to illustrate the process of determining cube roots using different methods.

Example

Calculate the cube root of 64 using estimation and prime factorization methods.

Estimation Method:

Start with an estimate, such as 4, and cube it: 4³ = 64. Since 64 matches the target, the cube root is 4.

Prime Factorization Method:

Prime factorize 64: 64 = 2² * 2² * 2². Since each factor appears three times, the cube root is 2 * 2 * 2 = 8.

Online calculators provide accessibility and convenience, especially for users without access to physical calculators.

Developers can write scripts to calculate cube roots programmatically, providing flexibility and customization options. This method is suitable for integrating cube root calculations into larger software applications or analysis workflows.

These technological tools offer diverse options for determining cube roots, catering to different preferences and requirements. Whether it's a quick calculation on a calculator or integrating cube root calculations into complex software systems, technology streamlines the process and enhances efficiency.
